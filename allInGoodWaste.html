All In Good Waste

Artificial Intelligence Garbage Sorter

GOAL: With the rapidly worsening state of our climate, alarmingly only 9% of plastic is accurately sorted. I strived to create a machine that precisely and efficiently sorts through trash. With the help of a Raspberry Pi camera and machine learning, the machine distinguishes between trash, paper, plastic, glass, cardboard, metal and transport the garbage to its correct bin, disposing of it properly.

IMPLEMENTATION: First, the camera takes a picture of the trash and using image processing as well as machine learning, it will identify the piece of trash. The display will show the output as well as the confidence level of the sorting. Along with that, the LEDâ€™s will light up to give another visual of what trash was identified as. Next, the NEMA17 motor will start moving to the location above its associated bin. The second motor will turn the cardboard, depositing the trash.

CONTRIBUTION: Having experimented with Arduinos in the past, I installed WinSCP and VNV viewer to communicate with the Raspberry PI, setting it up to the Wi-Fi, and downloading the 2500 pics of recycling from Lobe.AI. I was able to increase the accuracy of the machine from 80% to 94% with the help of Kaggle photos. Troubleshooting with fragile wires and attaching all necessary components, I was able to have LED light's successfully light up when a piece of trash was identified. Lastly, it was necessary for the Raspberry Pi and motors to communicate efficiently, thus depositing the trash into the correct bin.

HARDWARE COMPONENTS: Raspberry Pi, SD Card, USB-C Power Supply, Camera Board, Driver Chip, Bridge, and Raspberry Pi Kit (included Breadboard, Pushbuttons, 220 Ohm resistors, and jumper wires)
