Girls Empowerment Club

Co Founder/Co President

GOAL: Build a support system in the community, celebrate each other's successes and change the dynamic between girls to make a positive impact. I built a supportive, safe, and judgement-free space welcome to everyone. I encouraged diversity to promote real, relevant and significant social issues that are prevalant today.

Lee Whiteman & Kimberley Black Talk: Lee Whiteman is a professional Makeup Artist and Professor at Durham College for Cosmetology. She spoke to club members about confidence and delivered a self-esteem workshop. Kimberley Black, an assault survivor and Lee's friend joined us and spoke about the importance of having a positive outlook on life and bouncing back from any obstacles placed in your way.

STEM + GEC Collaboration: Noting the importance in making women feel welcomed and accepted in the STEM industry, the Girls Empowerment Club and STEM Club felt it was important to collaborate and encourage women to enter the field with support. The event consisted of relevant information and statistics to be shared while giving students the chance to have open communications about their apprehensions towards technology. Students were able to find mentors and excitedly learn more about chemistry through baking.

Black History Month: Throughout the month of February, the Girls Empowerment Club posted 3 inspiring black women weekly in efforts to educate students and promote discussion. The month terminated with a final presentation that summarized all the promoted individuals and spoke more about the heritage of Black History Month.
